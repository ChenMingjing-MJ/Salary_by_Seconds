How Cost of Living Affects Your Salary

When considering a job offer in a new city or evaluating a relocation opportunity, the raw salary number only tells part of the story. Cost of living varies dramatically between cities, meaning that a $100,000 salary in Houston, Texas provides a significantly different lifestyle than the same salary in New York City or San Francisco.

Cost-of-living adjustments account for differences in housing costs, groceries, transportation, healthcare, and general goods and services. For example, a $100,000 salary in Houston (COL index 65) would need to be approximately $154,000 in New York (COL index 100) to maintain the same standard of living. This is why many companies offer location-based pay bands.

Our salary comparison tool uses cost-of-living indices benchmarked against New York City (index = 100) to help you understand what your salary is truly worth across different cities worldwide. Use this data to negotiate relocation packages, evaluate remote work compensation, or plan international moves with confidence.

How to Calculate Your Take-Home Pay

Your take-home pay is the amount of money you actually receive after all taxes and deductions are subtracted from your gross salary. Understanding your net pay is crucial for budgeting, financial planning, and evaluating job offers. The difference between your gross salary and take-home pay can be substantial depending on your country, income level, and filing status.

Tax systems in most countries use progressive brackets, meaning different portions of your income are taxed at different rates. For example, in the United States, the first $11,600 of income (for single filers in 2024) is taxed at just 10%, while income above $609,350 is taxed at 37%. This means your effective tax rate is always lower than your marginal tax rate.

Our calculator supports tax brackets for the United States, United Kingdom, Australia, and Canada. Simply enter your gross annual salary, select your country and filing status, and instantly see your after-tax income broken down by year, month, biweekly period, week, and day.
